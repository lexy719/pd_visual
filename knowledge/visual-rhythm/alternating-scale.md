## alternating-scale
tags: rhythm, variety, editorial, visual-interest, layout, spacing

Alternate large, small, wide, dense and open sections so the page never settles into one cadence.

**Problem it solves** — equal-sized cards and repeated grids create fatigue; after three identical sections the reader stops seeing them.
**When to use it** — the page must hold interest across many sections, or the content is rich and dense enough to blur together.
**Structure** — Large reveal → Compact proof block → Wide feature panorama → Dense comparison or data block.
**Why it works** — contrast is what makes any single section legible as important. A page of uniform weight has no emphasis available to spend.
**Expected visual outcome** — the page reads as editorial rather than templated; section proportions visibly change, and the change tracks what matters.
**Examples** — a full-width hero followed by a compact testimonial strip, then an oversized feature canvas.

## measure-and-line-length
tags: typography, measure, readability, rhythm, spacing

Body text lives at 45-75 characters per line. Everything else is a consequence of that.

**Problem it solves** — full-width body copy on a wide screen is the most common readability failure on the web. The eye loses the line return, and the reader silently starts skimming.
**When to use it** — any paragraph text. Non-negotiable for long-form; still applies to a two-sentence supporting line.
**Structure** — Constrain body copy to a measure of roughly 65ch (45-75 acceptable) → let headlines run wider, since they are scanned rather than read → set line-height inversely to measure: tighter for short lines, looser (1.5-1.7) for long ones.
**Why it works** — the return sweep is the fragile moment in reading. Past ~75 characters the eye lands on the wrong line often enough to break comprehension; below ~45 it returns so often that rhythm never establishes.
**Expected visual outcome** — text columns look deliberately narrower than their container; the page reads as edited rather than poured in, and long sections stop feeling like work.
**Examples** — a full-bleed section whose paragraph is held to a centred measure while the headline spans wider.

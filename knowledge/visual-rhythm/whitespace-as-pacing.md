## whitespace-as-pacing
tags: spacing, whitespace, rhythm, pacing, layout

Space is the pacing instrument. Its distribution says what belongs together and what is about to change.

**Problem it solves** — uniform padding everywhere flattens a page into one long undifferentiated column. The reader gets no signal about where a thought ends and the next begins.
**When to use it** — always; most consequentially in premium/calm work, where space IS the argument the product is making.
**Structure** — Set section rhythm from one spacing scale → make between-section space clearly larger than within-section space → give the most important section the most air around it, not the most decoration inside it.
**Why it works** — grouping is read from proximity before content is read at all. Unequal space is what converts a stack of blocks into a sequence of ideas, and generous space around one element is the quietest possible way to say it matters.
**Expected visual outcome** — the page breathes and its structure stays legible at a glance, even squinting; the important section is obvious because of what surrounds it rather than what fills it.
**Examples** — a testimonial given a full screen of air where the surrounding sections are tightly packed.

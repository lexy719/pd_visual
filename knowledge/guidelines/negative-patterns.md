# Negative design knowledge

These rules are retrieval evidence, not universal bans. A planner must compare them against the product,
audience and chosen creative direction before using them.

## Luxury: protect restraint

tags: premium, luxury, editorial, avoid, color, motion, spacing

Avoid loud multi-stop gradients, a dense grid of equal-weight features, tiny spacing, or many competing font
weights. Avoid bouncy, constant motion. Luxury is communicated by editing: generous rhythm, one dominant idea,
and deliberate material detail. A cinematic hero may fit, but it must serve the product rather than act as a
generic spectacle.

## Enterprise: never trade clarity for novelty

tags: trustworthy, finance, corporate, avoid, layout, motion, accessibility

Avoid cartoon illustration styles, hidden primary navigation, giant kinetic display type, experimental scroll
mechanics, or copy that conceals the product's value. Enterprise visitors need orientation, proof, and fast paths
to action. Motion may support hierarchy but must not block scanning, keyboard use, or deep links.

## Product interfaces: do not disguise utility as a marketing reel

tags: minimal, technical, saas, product, avoid, layout, motion

Avoid scroll-jacking, autoplay media that delays the product, decorative panels with no information hierarchy, and
feature grids that repeat the same claim. A product site earns motion by revealing workflow, state change, or a
concrete before/after—not by adding a transition to every block.

## Cinematic storytelling: preserve an exit path

tags: premium, playful, aggressive, video, scroll, avoid, motion, accessibility

Avoid making a visitor wait for a video scene before they can understand the offer or navigate. Never make scroll
the only way to operate essential content. Every media-led scene needs a poster image, readable overlay contrast,
a reduced-motion version, and a mobile fallback that preserves the message without downloading a large video.

## High-energy brands: energy needs composition

tags: aggressive, playful, performance, avoid, color, typography, motion

Avoid equating energy with noise: five accent colors, every word animating, constant rotation, and competing
effects make a brand feel cheap rather than fast. Pick one kinetic principle—velocity, impact, interruption, or
rhythm—and use stillness around it so the moments of motion have weight.

## Decorative panels: a caption needs a subject

tags: image, media, panel, caption, avoid, universal, dead-zone

A captioned panel must contain an image or a real texture — an empty tinted rectangle with a caption
underneath reads as a loading failure, not as restraint, because readers have learned that shape from
broken pages. If a panel exists to hold imagery, it holds imagery; if the design calls for quiet, use
whitespace without a frame. Never ship a bordered or tinted box whose only content is its own label —
the caption promises a subject, and an absent subject breaks the promise in the most literal way.

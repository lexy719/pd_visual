## escalation
tags: narrative, escalation, trust, conversion, layout

Move from vision to proof to action, each section answering the next objection.

**Problem it solves** — a flat page feels generic because every section makes the same size of claim. Escalation gives the page somewhere to go.
**When to use it** — high-consideration offers where credibility must be built before the ask: SaaS, B2B, services.
**Structure** — Vision → Product → Proof → Testimonials → Pricing → Action, with each step deliberately more concrete than the last.
**Why it works** — the story runs desire → evidence → commitment. Every step lowers friction and raises confidence, so the ask lands on a reader who is already persuaded.
**Expected visual outcome** — the page reads as deliberate rather than repetitive; sections grow more concrete and specific closer to conversion.
**Examples** — a founder story, then product capabilities, then case studies, then pricing.

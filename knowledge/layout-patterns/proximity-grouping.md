## proximity-grouping
tags: layout, proximity, grouping, gestalt, spacing

Things that belong together sit together. Distance is the only grouping cue you need.

**Problem it solves** — labels drifting from their fields, captions equidistant between two images, headings floating between the section they close and the one they open. The reader groups by distance regardless of intent, and gets it wrong.
**When to use it** — any element that explains another: caption, label, helper text, section heading, price under a plan name.
**Structure** — Bind each element to its owner with visibly tighter space than the gap to anything else → keep the intra-group gap at most half the inter-group gap → never leave a heading equidistant between two blocks.
**Why it works** — proximity is the strongest grouping signal available and it is read pre-attentively, before content. A caption nearer the wrong image belongs to the wrong image, whatever it says.
**Expected visual outcome** — groups are unmistakable without boxes, lines or colour; the page reads as organised even at a glance, where no text is legible.
**Examples** — a section heading sitting tight to its content with a large gap above it; a price locked to its tier name rather than centred in the card.

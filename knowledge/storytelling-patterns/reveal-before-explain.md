## reveal-before-explain
tags: narrative, storytelling, reveal, curiosity, layout

Show the payoff before the explanation. Let people meet the value directly, then supply the detail.

**Problem it solves** — people skim explanatory copy and miss the value. Leading with the artifact creates curiosity and lowers cognitive load before any reading is asked for.
**When to use it** — the product has a clear visual payoff and the audience must grasp the experience fast.
**When NOT** — the value is abstract (a policy, a service, a guarantee) and is meaningless without framing first.
**Structure** — Hero → Product preview (the payoff section) → Benefits → Feature details.
**Why it works** — it matches how people learn: see first, understand second. The reveal earns the attention the explanation then spends.
**Expected visual outcome** — the page reads as immediate and confident; the opening section carries more visual weight than the copy that follows.
**Examples** — a product demo hero, a preview section, or a before/after comparison that lets the audience grasp the payoff instantly.

## objection-first
tags: narrative, storytelling, objection, trust, conversion, layout

Lead with the strongest reason not to buy, and answer it before anything else.

**Problem it solves** — the top objection is what the reader is already thinking. Deferring it to a footer FAQ means every section above is read through suspicion.
**When to use it** — one known objection dominates the decision: price, switching cost, lock-in, safety, "will this work for my case".
**When NOT** — there is no dominant objection. This pattern then manufactures a doubt that wasn't there.
**Structure** — The objection, named plainly → The honest answer → Supporting evidence → What it is good for, and what it isn't → Action.
**Why it works** — naming the hard thing first buys credibility cheaply. A brand trusted on its worst fact is trusted on its best ones, and the reader stops hunting for the catch.
**Expected visual outcome** — the page reads as candid and unusually direct; the opening is text-led rather than image-led, and quieter than a conventional hero.
**Examples** — a premium product opening on its price; a migration tool opening on switching cost.

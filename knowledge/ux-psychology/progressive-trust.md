## progressive-trust
tags: trust, persuasion, progressive, psychology, narrative, layout

Earn trust in stages rather than demanding confidence all at once.

**Problem it solves** — high-stakes or high-friction products lose people who are asked to commit before they believe. Each unanswered doubt compounds into the next.
**When to use it** — a strong value proposition that still needs reassurance before conversion: fintech, health, B2B, anything with switching cost.
**Structure** — Vision → Product → Proof → Testimonials → Pricing → Action. Each step answers one specific doubt, in the order it arises.
**Why it works** — trust compounds. A reader who has accepted the last claim is measurably more willing to accept the next; evidence arriving early is wasted on someone who hasn't yet cared.
**Expected visual outcome** — the page reads as measured and credible, becoming more evidence-dense and less rhetorical toward the end.
**Examples** — a story-led landing page that introduces the mission, then shows evidence, then social proof, then conversion.
